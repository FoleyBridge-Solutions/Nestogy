# Contract Management

Manage service agreements, SLAs, and contract lifecycles. Learn how to create contracts, define service levels, track compliance, and automate billing based on contract terms.

## Understanding Contracts

Contracts in Nestogy represent formal agreements between your MSP and your clients. They define:

- Services provided
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Pricing and billing terms
- Contract duration and renewal terms
- Scope of support

---

## Creating a Contract

To create a new service contract:

1. Navigate to **Contracts** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Contract**
3. Select the client
4. Configure contract details:
   - **Contract Name** - Descriptive name
   - **Contract Type** - MSP Agreement, Project, SLA, etc.
   - **Start Date** - When coverage begins
   - **End Date** - When coverage ends
   - **Auto-Renewal** - Automatic renewal settings
5. Define services and pricing
6. Set SLA terms
7. Click **Save**

---

## Contract Types

Different contract types for different scenarios:

### Managed Services Agreement (MSA)

Ongoing managed IT services:

- Monthly recurring charges
- Defined service scope
- Unlimited or bucket hours
- SLA commitments
- Regular business reviews

### Block Hours Contract

Pre-purchased time blocks:

- Client buys X hours upfront
- Draw down hours as used
- Hourly rate locked in
- Optional rollover of unused hours
- Expiration dates

### Project-Based Contract

Fixed-scope project work:

- One-time or milestone-based billing
- Defined deliverables
- Project timeline
- Success criteria
- Change order process

### Per-Device/Per-User Pricing

Scalable pricing model:

- Price per device or user
- Automatic billing adjustments
- Asset tracking integration
- Monthly reconciliation

## Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

Define and track service commitments:

### Response Time SLAs

How quickly you respond to issues:

- **Critical** - 15 minutes
- **High** - 1 hour
- **Medium** - 4 hours
- **Low** - 24 hours

### Resolution Time SLAs

How quickly you resolve issues:

- **Critical** - 4 hours
- **High** - 8 hours
- **Medium** - 3 business days
- **Low** - 5 business days

### Uptime SLAs

System availability commitments:

- Monthly uptime percentage (e.g., 99.9%)
- Downtime allowance calculations
- Maintenance window exclusions
- Penalty clauses for breaches

### SLA Tracking

Monitor SLA compliance:

- Real-time SLA status on tickets
- Warning indicators when approaching deadlines
- Automatic escalation for at-risk tickets
- Monthly SLA reports
- Client-facing SLA dashboards

## Covered Services

Define what's included in the contract:

### Service Categories

Organize services by type:

- **Helpdesk Support** - Remote assistance, phone/email support
- **On-Site Support** - Field technician visits
- **Monitoring & Maintenance** - Proactive system monitoring
- **Security Services** - Patch management, antivirus, backups
- **Strategic Services** - Planning, consulting, reviews

### Inclusions and Exclusions

Clearly define scope:

**Included:**
- Operating system support
- Business application support
- Network troubleshooting
- Email system support
- Regular maintenance

**Excluded:**
- Hardware procurement
- Software licensing costs
- Internet service provider issues
- Physical security
- Custom development

### Coverage Hours

Define when support is available:

- **24/7** - Around the clock coverage
- **Business Hours** - 8 AM - 6 PM weekdays
- **Extended Hours** - 7 AM - 9 PM weekdays
- **After-Hours** - Emergency-only coverage
- **Holiday Coverage** - Specify which holidays

## Pricing and Billing

Configure contract financial terms:

### Recurring Charges

Set up automatic monthly billing:

- Base monthly fee
- Per-user or per-device fees
- Additional service charges
- Automatic invoice generation
- Prorated billing for mid-month changes

### Included Hours

Define time allocations:

- **Unlimited** - All time included
- **Bucket Hours** - X hours per month included
- **Hybrid** - Base hours plus overage charges
- **Per-Incident** - Charge per ticket

### Overage Billing

Handle time beyond included hours:

- Overage rate per hour
- Automatic time tracking
- Monthly overage reports
- Client portal visibility
- Approval workflows for high overages

## Contract Amendments

Modify existing contracts:

### Change Orders

Document contract changes:

1. Create change order from contract page
2. Describe changes (scope, pricing, terms)
3. Get client approval (optional workflow)
4. Apply changes to contract
5. Generate amendment document

### Rate Adjustments

Update pricing over time:

- Annual rate increases
- Market adjustment clauses
- Volume discounts
- Multi-year price locks
- Grandfather pricing for long-term clients

## Contract Renewal

Manage contract lifecycle:

### Renewal Notifications

Automated renewal reminders:

- 90-day advance notice
- 60-day reminder
- 30-day final reminder
- Email notifications to client
- Internal team alerts

### Renewal Process

Renew expiring contracts:

1. Review contract performance
2. Propose rate adjustments
3. Update service scope if needed
4. Send renewal proposal
5. Execute new agreement
6. Update system with new terms

### Auto-Renewal

Set contracts to renew automatically:

- Automatic term extension
- Same terms or updated rates
- Client notification
- Opt-out window
- Seamless billing continuation

## Best Practices

Tips for effective contract management:

### Clear Documentation

- Write contracts in plain language
- Define all technical terms
- Include specific examples
- Document all inclusions and exclusions
- Keep contracts up to date

### Proactive Management

- Review contracts quarterly
- Track time usage patterns
- Monitor SLA compliance
- Schedule regular client reviews
- Address issues early

### Financial Health

- Ensure profitable pricing
- Track time accurately
- Monitor overages
- Adjust rates annually
- Review unprofitable contracts

### Client Communication

- Set clear expectations
- Provide regular updates
- Share performance reports
- Be transparent about overages
- Schedule business reviews
